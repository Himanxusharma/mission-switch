# How to read these notes

The materials are dense on purpose, but the **shape** is simple:

1. **Title & source** — where the extract came from (if you re-split from chat).
2. **At a glance** — a short on-ramp: what interviewers care about, a **talk track**, common mistakes, and a tiny checklist.
3. **The rest** — full explanations, diagrams, and question banks.

You don’t have to read top-to-bottom in one sitting. A calm rhythm is:

- **Monday–Friday:** one folder, **At a glance** + one deep subsection.  
- **Weekend:** one **system design** or **DSA** block + [cheatsheet](./interview-questions-cheatsheet.md) verbal drill.

Typography is intentionally **plain**: emphasis with **bold** and *italics*, horizontal rules as **breathing room**, and almost no emoji in the on-ramps so nothing fights for your attention.

If you regenerate files from `All_chats.md`, you can re-apply the on-ramp styling with:

`python3 scripts/beautify_playbook_sections.py`
